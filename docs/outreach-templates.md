# Outreach Templates

Use these as starting points. Always personalize with one concrete observation from the website.

## Cold Email 1

Subject: quick website note for {{business_name}}

Hi {{first_name_or_team}},

I was looking at {{business_name}} and noticed a few website issues that may be costing you inquiries, especially on mobile.

The main ones:
- {{issue_1}}
- {{issue_2}}
- {{issue_3}}

You already have demand coming in, so this is less about a visual refresh and more about making it easier for visitors to trust you and contact you.

If useful, I can send a short teardown with the highest-impact fixes and what a rebuild would improve.

Best,
{{your_name}}

## Contact Form Version

Hi, I took a quick look at your website and noticed a few issues that may be hurting conversions on mobile and making it harder for visitors to take the next step. If helpful, I can send a short teardown with the highest-impact fixes and what an updated rebuild would improve.

## Call Opener

Hi, is this the right place to reach whoever handles the website for {{business_name}}?

I was reviewing a few Syracuse-area business sites and noticed yours may be losing leads because of {{primary_issue}}. I do website rebuilds for local businesses and wanted to see who I should send a few quick notes to.

## Personalization Angles

- Mobile layout breaks or feels cramped
- Service pages are thin or hard to scan
- Slow homepage load
- No clear CTA above the fold
- Outdated trust signals or missing reviews / credentials
- Competitor site in Syracuse looks substantially stronger
- Contact path is harder than it should be

## Rules

- Lead with a business problem, not taste
- Use one or two concrete observations
- Keep first outreach under 120 words when possible
- Do not mention every flaw you found
- Ask for permission to send a teardown instead of forcing a pitch

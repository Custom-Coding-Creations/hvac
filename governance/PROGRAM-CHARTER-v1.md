# Program Charter v1

## Program
HVAC Website Performance Program (Potter-Perrone initial rollout)

## Purpose
Create a measurable, conversion-focused website system that raises qualified lead volume and booked jobs while protecting trust, accessibility, and delivery quality.

## Business Outcomes
- Increase lead volume by improving discoverability and conversion.
- Increase qualified lead rate by intent-based page structure and clearer CTAs.
- Increase booked jobs and close rate through lower-friction conversion paths.
- Improve revenue per visitor while reducing customer acquisition cost proxy.
- Establish a repeatable governance model for weekly operating decisions.

## Scope
- Governance and KPI operating model.
- Information architecture and conversion UX blueprint artifacts.
- Design-system handoff artifacts needed for implementation.
- Frontend template and component implementation for core pages.
- Validation evidence and traceability to Issue 2 and Issue 3 artifact sets.

## Out of Scope (This Increment)
- Full CI/CD hardening and deployment automation beyond baseline checks.
- Paid media strategy design and campaign execution.

## Cadence And Ownership
- Weekly Operating Review: Tuesday 10:00 ET, 45 minutes.
- Accountable Owner: Program Lead.
- KPI Reporting Owner: Analytics Lead.
- Build Quality Owner: Frontend Lead.
- Go/No-Go Authority: Program Sponsor plus Frontend Lead (joint sign-off).

## Decision Rights
- Scope and KPI changes: Program Sponsor approval required.
- Design-system deviations: Design Owner and Frontend Lead approval required.
- Launch/no-launch: Go/No-Go Authority, based on measurable gate thresholds.

## Dependencies
- Issue 2 artifact set: IA, journeys, conversion blueprint, wireframe specs.
- Issue 3 artifact set: tokens, component states, accessibility matrix, QA checklist.
- Issue 4 stream: architecture and release operations remain parallel dependency.

## Success Criteria
- KPI baseline sheet includes formula, owner, baseline, 30-day target, 90-day target.
- Weekly review and escalation protocol are actively used.
- Core templates provide at least two conversion paths each.
- Accessibility and responsive QA pass defined gates.

## Timeline (Initial)
- Week 1: Governance package complete and approved.
- Week 2: IA and design-system artifact bootstrap complete.
- Week 3-4: Frontend primitives, templates, conversion components.
- Week 5: QA evidence, traceability, go/no-go review.

## Escalation Tree
1. Execution blocker identified by any owner.
2. Program Lead triage within one business day.
3. If unresolved, escalate to Program Sponsor within 24 hours.
4. If launch risk is material, mandatory no-go review within same day.

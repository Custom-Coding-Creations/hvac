Allergy and asthma control begins at home. Many people with allergies stay indoors when pollen and mold is high. But dust mites, pet dander and even cockroaches can cause problems indoors.<\/p>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

The\u00a0Environmental Protection Agency recommends three ways you can improve indoor air quality:<\/p>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

    \r\n
  • Control your contact with indoor airborne allergens<\/li>\r\n
  • Ventilate your indoor areas well<\/li>\r\n
  • Use air cleaners to clean indoor air<\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

    Eight out of 10 people in the United States are exposed to dust mites. Six out of 10 are exposed to cat or dog dander. Cockroaches cause allergic reactions in people who live in the inner cities or southern parts of the United States.\u00a0<\/p>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

    Better air quality in your home, office, school\u00a0and car can reduce allergy and asthma triggers.<\/p>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

    What Size Are Allergens?<\/h3>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

    Allergens are substances that cause allergic reactions and\u00a0trigger asthma symptoms<\/a>\u00a0in some people. The air spreads allergens around. They settle onto furniture and floors. They vary in size and are measured in microns (also called micrometers).<\/p>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

    Is It Possible to Control Indoor Allergens?<\/h3>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

    You can control indoor allergens with cleaning and reducing allergens in your home. The main sources of indoor allergens are:<\/p>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

      \r\n
    • Pets<\/li>\r\n
    • Wall-to-wall carpet<\/li>\r\n
    • Soft furniture<\/li>\r\n
    • Stuffed toys<\/li>\r\n
    • Bedding<\/li>\r\n
    • Damp areas<\/li>\r\n
    • Indoor plants<\/li>\r\n
    • Mattresses that aren\u2019t in allergy covers<\/li>\r\n
    • Pillows and bedding you can\u2019t wash in hot water<\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

      There may be more allergens on surfaces than in the air. Surface allergens enter the air easily when you disturb them by dusting or sitting.<\/p>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

1. Dirty Air Filters<\/b><\/h2>\r\n

This one\u2019s the<\/span> A\/C system\u2019s worst enemy<\/b><\/a>\u2014by far. When your air filter is clogged with dust, pet hair, and pollen, your system has to work overtime to push air through\u2014and that means higher bills, weaker airflow, and a shorter system lifespan.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\r\n

How to prevent it:<\/b><\/p>\r\n

    \r\n
  • Switch out air filters every 1-3 months, <\/b>especially in summer<\/span><\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n
      \r\n
    • If you have pets or struggle with allergies, check it monthly<\/span><\/li>\r\n
    • Inspect them regularly for any debris buildup<\/span><\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n

      Pro tip:<\/b> Hold your filter up to the light\u2014if you can\u2019t see through it, <\/span>it\u2019s time for a new one.<\/b><\/p>\r\n

       <\/p>\r\n

      2. Low Refrigerant Levels<\/b><\/h2>\r\n
        \r\n
      • \r\n

        Your A\/C cools your home by removing heat using refrigerant. If refrigerant levels drop (<\/span>often due to leaks<\/span><\/a>),<\/span> your system will struggle to cool<\/b>\u2014and could even suffer costly compressor damage.<\/span><\/p>\r\n

        Signs of a refrigerant issue:<\/b><\/p>\r\n

          \r\n
        • Vents blowing warm air<\/span><\/li>\r\n
        • Hissing or bubbling noises near your unit<\/span><\/li>\r\n
        • Higher electric bills<\/span><\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n

          How to prevent it:<\/b><\/p>\r\n

            \r\n
          • Schedule annual maintenance to <\/span>check refrigerant levels<\/b> and detect leaks early<\/span><\/li>\r\n
          • If you suspect a leak, <\/span>call a professional immediately<\/b><\/a>\u2014refrigerant is not a DIY fix<\/span>

            <\/span><\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n<\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n

            3. Faulty Thermostats<\/b><\/h2>\r\n

            Your A\/C might be innocent… the thermostat could be the sneaky culprit. <\/b>If it\u2019s outdated, inaccurate, or not communicating properly, you could be facing uneven cooling or a unit that won\u2019t run at all.<\/span><\/p>\r\n

            Watch out for:<\/b><\/p>\r\n

              \r\n
            • \r\n
                \r\n
              • A\/C short cycling<\/span><\/li>\r\n
              • Inaccurate room temperatures<\/span><\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n<\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n
                  \r\n
                • Blank or unresponsive screen<\/span>\u00a0<\/b><\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n

                    \r\n
                  • Upgrade to <\/span>smart thermostat<\/span><\/a><\/li>\r\n
                  • Let a Heat Wiser tech test your thermostat during your next tune-up for accuracy<\/span><\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n

                    Cool <\/i><\/b>fact: <\/b>Smart thermostats can reduce cooling costs and give you remote control via smartphone!<\/span><\/p>\r\n

                     <\/p>\r\n

                    4. Blocked Condenser Units<\/b><\/h2>\r\n

                    Your outdoor condenser unit is essential for releasing heat\u2014but it can\u2019t do its job if it\u2019s blocked by debris or overgrowth.<\/span><\/p>\r\n

                    Check monthly for:<\/b><\/p>\r\n

                      \r\n
                    • Leaves, branches, or grass clippings<\/span><\/li>\r\n
                    • Weeds and overgrown shrubs<\/span><\/li>\r\n
                    • Outdoor furniture or storage bins<\/span><\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n

                      General rule-of thumb: <\/b>Always<\/span> keep at least<\/span> 2 feet of space<\/b> around your outdoor unit<\/span><\/p>\r\n

                      • Buzzing or clicking noises<\/span><\/li>\r\n
                      • A burning smell<\/span><\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n

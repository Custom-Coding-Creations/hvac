The HVAC system also comprises a huge part of your energy bills.<\/p>\r\n

\u00a0One of the largest energy loads in your household is cooling or heating your home. According to the U.S. Department of Energy (DoE), about 6% of the total electricity generated in the United States is used for air conditioning and heating. Now, how can you save up on energy bills if you frequently use your HVAC system? One such way is by getting a HVAC upgrade in New York.<\/p>\r\n

\u00a0While you might think that getting your HVAC system upgraded is expensive, in reality, it will help you save more over time. Think of it as an investment, where you get a system that helps regulate your energy consumption\u2013so you\u2019ll still get to use your heating and cooling equipment regularly but without the spike in energy bills. You can see this in your equipment\u2019s SEER rating.<\/p>\r\n

What Is a SEER Rating?<\/h2>\r\n

In order to understand energy efficiency, you need to know and understand how energy efficiency is measured. In heating and cooling equipment, this is indicated by a system\u2019s Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio, or SEER. This SEER is measured by comparing the cooling output of a system over a typical cooling season to the amount of energy input the system consumes over the same time frame. The higher the SEER value, the more energy-efficient the system is.<\/p>\r\n

\u00a0In recent times, the average SEER rating of an air conditioner is between 13 and 14, though some systems can reach as high as the low-20s for ultra-efficient models. But around 15 to 20 years ago, the average SEER rating of a system on the market was around 10. If your system is approaching two decades in age, then there\u2019s a good chance that this was probably the original SEER rating for your system. It might be time for a change. Upgrading your HVAC system will definitely improve your equipment\u2019s SEER rating.<\/p>\r\n

Immediate Steps to Take When Your Heat Fails<\/b><\/h2>\r\n

When your heater stops working, it\u2019s natural to worry. Taking these simple steps can make a big difference while waiting for professional help:<\/span><\/p>\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

Check Your Thermostat and Power<\/b><\/h3>\r\n
    \r\n
  • Make sure the thermostat is set to \u201cheat\u201d and that the temperature is higher than the current room temperature.<\/span><\/li>\r\n
  • Replace the thermostat batteries to rule out a communication issue.<\/span><\/li>\r\n
  • Check your circuit breaker and reset it if tripped. If it trips again immediately, stop and call a professional<\/a>.<\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n

    Reset Your Furnace<\/b><\/h3>\r\n
      \r\n
    • Turn off the furnace power switch (located near the unit) or reset the breaker. Wait a minute before turning it back on to see if the system restarts.<\/span><\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n

      Replace the Furnace Filter<\/strong><\/h3>\r\n

      A clogged filter can cause the furnace to shut down as a safety measure. If your filter is dirty, replace it, and try restarting the furnace.<\/span><\/p>\r\n

      Check the Pilot Light<\/strong><\/h3>\r\n

      For gas furnaces, ensure the pilot light is on. If it\u2019s out, follow the manufacturer\u2019s instructions to relight it, or <\/span>call a professional for assistance<\/span><\/a>.<\/span><\/p>\r\n

      Gather in a Warm Space<\/strong><\/h3>\r\n

      Close doors to unused rooms and seal drafts around windows and doors with towels or blankets to retain heat.<\/span><\/p>\r\n

      Use Temporary Heat Safely<\/h3>\r\n

      Use <\/span>portable heaters with safety features<\/span><\/a> like tip-over protection, keeping them away from flammable materials.<\/span><\/p>\r\n

      What to Do While Waiting for Professional Help<\/b><\/h2>\r\n

      While waiting for <\/span>Heat Wiser\u2019s 24\/7 emergency service<\/span><\/a> to arrive, focus on staying warm and safe:<\/span><\/p>\r\n

      Prevent Frozen Pipes<\/strong><\/h3>\r\n
        \r\n
      • Let faucets drip to keep water moving and prevent pipes from freezing.<\/span><\/li>\r\n
      • Open cabinet doors under sinks to allow warm air to circulate around plumbing.<\/span><\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n

        Stay Warm and Layer Up<\/strong><\/h3>\r\n
          \r\n
        • Dress in warm layers, use blankets, and wear hats and socks to conserve body heat.<\/span><\/li>\r\n
        • Keep your family and pets in one room to share warmth.<\/span><\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n

          Monitor for Carbon Monoxide<\/strong><\/h3>\r\n
            \r\n
          • Never use outdoor appliances, like grills or generators, indoors for heat.<\/span><\/li>\r\n
          • Ensure your carbon monoxide detectors<\/a> are functioning properly. Call 911 and leave your home immediately if they alarm.<\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n

            What Qualifies as a Heating Emergency?<\/b><\/h2>\r\n

            Not every heating issue requires immediate action, but the following situations should be addressed right away:<\/span><\/p>\r\n

              \r\n
            • No Heat at All:<\/b> If your furnace won\u2019t start and temperatures are freezing, this is an emergency.<\/span><\/li>\r\n
            • Cold Air from Vents:<\/b> A <\/span>furnace blowing cold air<\/span><\/a> may indicate ignition or airflow problems that need professional attention.<\/span><\/li>\r\n
            • Unusual Smells:<\/b> A gas smell or burning odor could signal a gas leak or electrical issue\u2013 shut off the system and <\/span>call a professional immediately<\/span><\/a>.<\/span><\/li>\r\n
            • Strange Noises:<\/b> Banging, grinding, or squealing sounds suggest mechanical issues that could worsen if ignored.<\/span><\/li>\r\n
            • Frozen Pipes:<\/b> If your home becomes too cold, frozen and burst pipes can lead to water damage.<\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n

              Safety First: Heating Tips for Peace of Mind<\/h2>\r\n
                \r\n
              1. Schedule Regular Maintenance. <\/b>Avoid breakdowns by <\/span>scheduling a <\/span>$99 seasonal tune-up<\/b><\/a> to keep your furnace running efficiently.<\/span><\/li>\r\n
              2. Know When to Call. <\/b>Don\u2019t hesitate to <\/span>call for professional help<\/span><\/a> if you\u2019re unsure or the issue persists\u2013 peace of mind is worth it.<\/span><\/li>\r\n
              3. Be Prepared. <\/b>Keep blankets, space heaters, and backup batteries for thermostats on hand for emergencies.<\/li>\r\n<\/ol>\r\n

You lower the thermostat and wait for that refreshing blast of cool air \u2026 But instead, all you get is a wave of warm air filling the room.\u00a0<\/b><\/p>\r\n

Something\u2019s not right if your AC is pumping out warm air, and it could be an early sign of a more serious problem.<\/span><\/p>\r\n

At<\/span> Heat Wiser<\/span><\/a>, we understand how frustrating it can be when your cooling system isn\u2019t performing as it should. Here\u2019s a closer look at what might be causing it to blow warm air and how to get things back on track.<\/span><\/p>\r\n

\u00a0<\/span>1. Your Thermostat Settings Need a Second Look<\/b><\/h2>\r\n

It sounds simple, but one of the first things to check is your thermostat. Sometimes, it\u2019s just a case of <\/span>accidental settings.<\/span><\/a><\/p>\r\n

Things to check:<\/b><\/p>\r\n

    \r\n
  • Is the thermostat set to \u201ccool\u201d instead of \u201cheat\u201d or \u201cfan\u201d?<\/span><\/li>\r\n
  • Is the temperature set lower than the current room temperature?<\/span><\/li>\r\n
  • Have the batteries been changed recently?<\/span><\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n

    What it means:<\/span><\/i> If your thermostat isn\u2019t correctly communicating with your system, your AC can\u2019t cool properly. This is a quick fix, but it\u2019s often overlooked.<\/span><\/p>\r\n

    2. Low Refrigerant Levels or a Leak<\/b><\/h2>\r\n

    Refrigerant is the lifeblood of your air conditioning system. If your system is low on refrigerant from a leak or because it wasn\u2019t filled properly, it prevents your AC from absorbing heat and makes it blow out warm air instead.<\/span><\/p>\r\n

    Signs of low refrigerant:<\/b><\/p>\r\n

      \r\n
    • Reduced cooling efficiency<\/span><\/li>\r\n
    • Ice buildup on the indoor or outdoor coil<\/span><\/li>\r\n
    • Hissing or bubbling noises from the unit<\/span><\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n

      What it means:<\/span><\/i> A refrigerant issue is not something to ignore. Left unchecked, it can damage the compressor and lead to a much costlier repair. This is a job for a certified HVAC technician, and<\/span> Heat Wiser is happy to help!<\/span><\/a><\/p>\r\n

      3. Dirty or Clogged Air Filters<\/b><\/h2>\r\n

      Your AC needs clean, unrestricted airflow to work properly. A clogged air filter can block that airflow, making your system work harder, and ultimately leading to poor cooling performance.<\/span><\/p>\r\n

      What to look for:<\/b><\/p>\r\n

        \r\n
      • Reduced airflow from vents<\/span><\/li>\r\n
      • Increased dust in the home<\/span><\/li>\r\n
      • AC cycles more frequently than usual<\/span><\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n

        What it means:<\/span><\/i> If your air filter hasn\u2019t been changed in over a month, it\u2019s time. In fact, <\/span>we recommend checking it monthly,<\/span><\/a> especially during the summer months.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\r\n

        4. Electrical Issues or a Tripped Breaker<\/b><\/h2>\r\n

        Air conditioning systems rely on multiple electrical components working together. If your outdoor condenser unit loses power due to a tripped breaker, faulty capacitor, or wiring problem, the cooling cycle can\u2019t be completed properly.<\/span><\/p>\r\n

        How to identify the problem:<\/b><\/p>\r\n

          \r\n
        • Indoor fan runs, but no cool air comes out<\/span><\/li>\r\n
        • Outdoor unit isn\u2019t making noise or spinning<\/span><\/li>\r\n
        • Circuit breaker keeps tripping<\/span><\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n

          What it means:<\/span><\/i> Your indoor air handler may still be running, but without the condenser operating, warm air is all you\u2019ll ever get. Fixing it could be as easy as resetting the breaker, or it might call for <\/span>expert repair.<\/span><\/a><\/p>\r\n

          5. Dirty Condenser Coils<\/b><\/h2>\r\n

          Your outdoor unit releases the heat pulled from your home. If those condenser coils are covered in dirt, grass, or debris, they can\u2019t do their job effectively.<\/span><\/p>\r\n

          Common signs:<\/b><\/p>\r\n

            \r\n
          • AC runs constantly but doesn\u2019t cool<\/span><\/li>\r\n
          • Unit feels hot to the touch<\/span><\/li>\r\n
          • Higher than normal energy bills<\/span><\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n

            What it means:<\/span><\/i> A dirty condenser coil reduces system efficiency and increases wear on components. While professional cleaning during seasonal maintenance is essential, doing some <\/span>simple DIY upkeep <\/span><\/a>between your biannual tune-ups can make a big difference. It\u2019s a lot easier than you think!<\/span><\/p>\r\n

            6. Leaky or Disconnected Ductwork<\/b><\/h2>\r\n

            In some cases, the AC is cooling properly, but that cool air isn\u2019t reaching your rooms. Leaky ducts or disconnected joints can let conditioned air escape into your attic or crawl space.<\/span><\/p>\r\n

            Things to keep an eye on:<\/b><\/p>\r\n

              \r\n
            • Warm or uneven temperatures in certain rooms<\/span><\/li>\r\n
            • Whistling noises from vents<\/span><\/li>\r\n
            • Higher utility bills without explanation<\/span><\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n

              What it means:<\/span><\/i> Ductwork issues are especially common in older Syracuse homes.<\/span> A duct inspection from Heat Wiser<\/span><\/a> can identify and fix these problems to restore full system performance.<\/span><\/p>\r\n

              7. Your System Is Due for a Tune-Up<\/b><\/h2>\r\n

              The secret to a well-functioning AC is consistent preventative maintenance. During a tune-up, a technician can catch small issues before they turn into major breakdowns.<\/span><\/p>\r\n

              Maintenance includes:<\/b><\/p>\r\n

                \r\n
              • Cleaning the coils and filters<\/span><\/li>\r\n
              • Checking refrigerant levels<\/span><\/li>\r\n
              • Inspecting electrical connections<\/span><\/li>\r\n
              • Verifying airflow and thermostat calibration<\/span><\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n

                What it means:<\/span><\/i> Haven\u2019t had an <\/span>AC tune-up in over a year? <\/span><\/a>Don\u2019t wait any longer. Regular service can help your system last longer and cool more effectively, especially during hot NY summers.<\/span><\/p>\r\n

                8. The System Is Old or Undersized<\/b><\/h2>\r\n

                Even well-maintained AC systems wear out eventually. If your unit is more than <\/span>10\u201320 years old <\/span><\/a>or not properly sized, it may no longer meet your cooling needs.<\/span><\/p>\r\n

                Signs of an aging unit:<\/b><\/p>\r\n

                  \r\n
                • Frequent breakdowns or repairs<\/span><\/li>\r\n
                • High energy bills<\/span><\/li>\r\n
                • Inconsistent temperatures throughout the home<\/span><\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\r\n

                  What it means:<\/span><\/i> It might be time to consider replacing your unit. Heat Wiser offers <\/span>free estimates on system installations<\/span><\/a> and can help you choose an energy-efficient model that\u2019s right for your home and budget.<\/span><\/p>\r\n

Why Subzero Temperatures Make Furnace Failures More Dangerous<\/b><\/h2>\n

When outdoor <\/span>temperatures drop below zero<\/span><\/a>, indoor heat loss accelerates. Even a well-insulated home can lose several degrees every hour without a functioning furnace.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

That rapid temperature drop can pose the risk of <\/span>hypothermia and frostbite.<\/span><\/a><\/p>\n

Frozen or burst pipes are another major concern. A single burst pipe can cause thousands of dollars in damage, far more than the cost of a <\/span>furnace repair.<\/span><\/a><\/p>\n

Being prepared isn\u2019t optional during extreme cold\u2014it\u2019s essential.<\/span><\/p>\n

Contact Heat Wiser as Soon as the Heat Goes Out<\/b><\/h2>\n

If your furnace quits, don\u2019t wait to see if it \u201ckicks back on.\u201d In subzero temperatures, your home can lose heat quickly, and what starts as a small malfunction can escalate into a major repair within hours.<\/span><\/p>\n

The moment you notice the heat is off, <\/span>contact us immediately<\/b><\/a>. We offer 24\/7 emergency HVAC service across Syracuse and CNY, so you\u2019re never left facing extreme cold alone.<\/span><\/p>\n

While our team is on the way, use the tips below to stay warm with safe backup heat sources and simple heat-preserving steps. Even small actions can make a big difference while you wait.<\/span><\/p>\n

    \n
  1. Keep Backup Heat Sources Ready (and Safe)<\/b><\/li>\n<\/ol>\n

    Every home in NY should have at least one safe, supplemental heat source available.<\/span><\/p>\n

    Recommended: Infrared Space Heaters<\/b><\/h3>\n

    Infrared heaters<\/span><\/a> provide quick, efficient heat and are ideal during furnace outages. Heat Wiser recommends keeping at least two infrared space heaters on hand, especially for long winter nights.<\/span><\/p>\n

    For safety:<\/b><\/p>\n

      \n
    • Choose models with tip-over shutoff and overheat protection<\/span><\/li>\n
    • Keep heaters three feet away from furniture, curtains, and bedding<\/span><\/li>\n
    • Always place them on a stable, nonflammable surface<\/span><\/li>\n
    • Never leave them running unattended<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      Fireplaces (Gas or Wood)<\/b><\/h3>\n

      If your home has a functional fireplace, it can provide valuable supplemental heat. Gas fireplaces deliver steady warmth instantly, while wood-burning units give strong radiant heat that can keep a main living area comfortable.<\/span><\/p>\n

      To <\/b>prevent hazards<\/b><\/a>:<\/b><\/p>\n

        \n
      • Ensure the flue is fully open before starting a fire.<\/span><\/li>\n
      • Burn only approved materials, such as seasoned wood, manufactured logs, or your gas unit\u2019s proper fuel source.<\/span><\/li>\n
      • Use a screen or glass doors to contain sparks.<\/span><\/li>\n
      • Never leave a fire unattended, and fully extinguish wood fires before bed.<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

        Avoid These Unsafe Heat Sources<\/b><\/h3>\n

        Some heating \u201cshortcuts\u201d do more harm than good, especially during subzero weather.<\/span><\/p>\n

          \n
        • Never use your oven or stovetop for heat. They aren\u2019t designed for home heating and can create <\/span>fire hazards or carbon monoxide buildup.<\/span><\/a><\/li>\n
        • Don\u2019t use kerosene heaters indoors unless they\u2019re specifically rated for indoor use and kept in a well-ventilated space.<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

          Backup heat is only meant to get you through the moment\u2014not replace your furnace. Use it cautiously, and <\/span>call Heat Wiser right away<\/b> <\/a>so we can restore your heat safely and quickly.<\/span><\/p>\n

          2. Keep Warm Air Where You Need It<\/b><\/h2>\n

          When the heat goes out<\/span><\/a>, your goal is to retain as much warmth as possible.<\/span><\/p>\n

          Here\u2019s how:<\/span><\/p>\n

            \n
          • Close unused rooms to trap heat in the areas you\u2019re using.<\/span><\/li>\n
          • Seal drafts<\/span><\/a> with towels, blankets, or draft stoppers.<\/span><\/li>\n
          • Cover windows with heavy curtains or blankets to slow heat loss.<\/span><\/li>\n
          • Layer up with warm clothing, socks, and hats.<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

            Even small adjustments can help you feel several degrees warmer.<\/span><\/p>\n

            3. Protect Vulnerable Family Members and Pets<\/b><\/h2>\n

            During a furnace outage, especially in subzero temperatures, some family members are at higher risk of cold-related health issues.<\/span><\/p>\n

            Pay close attention to:<\/b><\/p>\n

              \n
            • Babies and young children<\/span><\/li>\n
            • Elderly family members<\/span><\/li>\n
            • Pets<\/span><\/a><\/li>\n
            • Anyone who is medically fragile<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

              Keep them closest to your supplemental heating source and provide extra layers, blankets, or sleeping bags.<\/span><\/p>\n

              4. Stay Active to Generate Body Heat<\/b><\/h2>\n

              Movement helps maintain circulation and keeps you warmer.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

              Indoor activity ideas include:<\/b><\/p>\n

                \n
              • Light stretching<\/span><\/li>\n
              • Indoor chores<\/span><\/li>\n
              • Walking around your home<\/span><\/li>\n
              • Playing with kids or pets<\/span><\/li>\n
              • Simple exercises like marching in place<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

                It won\u2019t replace your furnace, but it will keep your body temperature from dropping too quickly.<\/span><\/p>\n

                5. Prepare Before the Next Cold Snap Hits<\/b><\/h2>\n

                Most heating emergencies are <\/span>preventable with routine care<\/span><\/a>. Some simple steps can dramatically reduce your odds of losing heat during the next subzero spell.<\/span><\/p>\n

                Here are some simple steps to take:\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

                  \n
                • Schedule <\/span>annual furnace maintenance<\/span><\/a> to catch issues early and boost reliability.<\/span><\/li>\n
                • Replace air filters every 1\u20133 months.<\/span><\/li>\n
                • Clear snow and ice from outdoor units<\/span><\/li>\n
                • Consider upgrading<\/span><\/a> older, struggling systems<\/span><\/li>\n
                • Invest in a smart thermostat<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

                  Being proactive is the most effective way to avoid a midnight emergency call.<\/span><\/p>\n

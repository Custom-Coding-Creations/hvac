## SECTION 9: OBJECTIONS & RESPONSES

### Objection 1: "Our business is built on referrals. We don't need a website."

**Response:**
"Referrals are capped by your existing customer base. A modernized website doesn't replace referrals—it multiplies them by:
- Attracting new customers to your referral network
- Making it easier for existing customers to refer (shareable pages)
- Building online reputation (reviews, testimonials) that justifies referrals

Plus, referral sourcing has become increasingly expensive. A $25K website investment that generates $1.3M+ in revenue is cheaper than relying solely on referrals."

### Objection 2: "We tried a website redesign before; it didn't help."

**Response:**
"That redesign likely failed because:
1. The design was updated but UX/conversion strategy wasn't (just a facelift)
2. No lead capture optimization (no chat, scheduling, or CTA testing)
3. Poor mobile experience (most visitors from mobile; if that wasn't optimized, the redesign failed)
4. No ongoing optimization (launched and forgot)

Modern redesigns fail 40% of the time because they're purely cosmetic. We recommend a strategy-first approach: messaging, UX, conversion funnels, then design."

### Objection 3: "Our customers don't search online. They call or ask neighbors."

**Response:**
"Your customers might not search, but they do:
- Search for emergency plumbing/HVAC when pipes burst at 2 AM

- Look you up on Google Maps while they wait for your arrival

Your website is a trust filter, even if it's not generating conscious leads. A modern site converts those casual visitors into confirmed leads."

### Objection 4: "This costs too much."

**Response:**
"The website costs $25K one-time + $8.5K annual.
The revenue impact is $1.3M annually (conservative estimate).
ROI is 5,100%.
Payback period is 9 days.

Compare this to other Potter-Perrone investments:

### Objection 5: "Heat Wiser isn't a direct competitor; we serve different markets."
